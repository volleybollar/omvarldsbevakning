Stanford study: AI writing feedback tools show racial and gender bias in student evaluations
Stanford researchers fed 600 middle school essays into four AI models with varied student identity descriptions and found consistent 'positive feedback bias' and 'feedback withholding bias' — more praise and less criticism offered to some student groups based on race, gender, motivation, and disability status. The paper, nominated for best paper at LAK26, found these patterns were consistent across all four AI systems tested. The study warns that AI writing feedback tools could steer students toward divergent learning outcomes depending on their demographic identity.
