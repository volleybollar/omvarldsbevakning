Study: Teachers using AI tutoring tools tend to repeatedly help the same students
A new NC State University study finds that when teachers use AI-powered intelligent tutoring systems, they tend to assist the same subset of students rather than distributing attention evenly. Analyzing 1.4 million interactions from 339 students across 14 math classes, researchers found that a student's prior help history and current engagement state were the strongest predictors of receiving teacher attention. The findings, presented at the LAK26 conference in Bergen, Norway, could inform tools that help teachers track and equalize their classroom interactions.
